Bishop's Tavern is the sort of place I would typically avoid, preferring any one of the number of independent pubs up Gloucester road over a Greene King . However, I spotted a dartboard through the window and therefore decided to make a trip. The board is next to a large window, with the oche parallel to the pavement outside. It's bright and a good spot for people watching. The beer choice was a standard selection of lagers and IPAs, there was nothing on cask when I visited, I'm not sure if that's standard.

The board is a fairly cheap Winmau Pro SFB. It has round wires, however, SFB stands for staple free board, meaning fewer bounce outs than the cheapest boards. It's also in decent nick, with no worn out segments or hard spots. I only played in the daylight, but there is a dedicated light above which I'm sure would illuminate the board adequately.

The oche is a mat, but it is broader and flatter than the typical mat you see. It also has the nice addition of having a checkout chart. This is particularly useful for intermediate players looking to improve their route to a double. I have previously threatened to carpet my house with checkout chart mat so I'll never be without a checkout route again. The checkout table is particularly comprehenisive, with routes for three darts from 170 and routes for two darts from 110. As usual it has the steel tip and soft tip oche lengths. I didn't measure the length, but the board was the correct height and the mat was flush below the board.

There is a chalkboard to the left of the board, suitably far away that one could score without being in danger from another player throwing darts. It's also a good sized board, meaning low scorers such as myself don't need to write particularly small. Also, plenty of space for a big game of half it. When I visited it was being used by Melissa to announce her birthday.

src="/images/bishops-tavern/chalkboard.jpg"

All in all it's a nice dedicated darts area. When I have walked past on nice days in the summer the windows are fully opened, which I assume make it a pleasant place to throw some arrows. However, there are often a lot of people smoking outside, so it may just transport you back to pre 2005. I'm also not sure if you could have a casual throw at busy times, certainly when big sports games are on the place fills up including the oche.
